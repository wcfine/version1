Importance of Financial literacy For Students and Teens


Hi everyone! I am sure that we all have received some money from our parents as pocket money and have been asked to save that for future, haven’t we? Parents do this so that we learn the concept of saving and the importance of money. And have you ever heard of the term finance, sure you did. First thing that comes to our mind is stock market and how it is risky and also that we can’t learn that, well it is actually not the truth.

So in today’s blog we will be learning about finance from a student’s perspective. In this blog we only learn about the importance of finance for students. But In the following blogs I will be sharing about the basic concepts of it.

First step, what is FINANCE? Finance is a term for the management, creation, and study of money and investments. Financial literacy means knowing how to manage your money effectively. It is not as hard as we think. Students can learn it too. But the question is why? Why should we?

To start, I would suggest every student to read the book “Rich Dad, Poor Dad”. This book changed my way of looking at personal finance. It is all about importance of money and why should we invest it. Wait a minute, this is a new term- ‘Invest. What does it mean? Investing your money means act of allocating money, with the expectation of generating an income or profit. We’ll learn about this later.
In “Rich Dad, Poor Dad”, Robert T Kiyoski tells us that we don’t have to work for money instead make money work for us by investing it in different assets. Over the time, that money will grow! The book also states that if we (students) learn about this then we won’t have to suffer financially when we grow up. Because we will already know how to manage money make it work for us.

So in this blog we looked at finance from student’s perspective and why is it important being financially literate when we are a student. Hope you all learned something from this blog and the upcoming blogs will be about the basic concepts of finance and then later we will learn about investment.



Xenobots

Xenobots are named after the African clawed frog. They are synthetic life forms designed by computers to do some desired function and built by combining together different biological tissues.

We pronounce xenobots as “zeeno-bots”. Xenobots were first made(born) when scientists extracted skin stem cells from frog embryos and cultured them in salt water.
There is still a debate going on – whether xenobots are robots or organisms or something else. The first xenobots were built by Douglas Blackiston according to blueprints generated by an AI program, which was developed by Sam Kriegman.
They are used in medicine and environmental remediation. Their basic and main components are frog cells. They were invented in 2020. They can persist for weeks without food and heal themselves from lacerations (Cuts or skin wound).



A xenobot is about 0.039 inches (less than 1 millimetre) wide. It is composed of just two things: Skin cells and heart muscle cells. xenobots can also self-replicate. xenobots can gather loose cells in their environment forming them into new xenobots with the same capability. Xenobots can walk, swim, push pellets and carry payloads.
Baby xenobots were created by a process called kinetic replication- phenomenon never

observed before but known to happen at the molecular level. the best possible way to explain a xenobot is by calling it a "living, programmable organism." Xenobots reproduce by scooping up free-floating cells and assembling them into new clusters.


These xenobots have some advantages as well as some disadvantages.

Advantages:


1. They could carry out tasks inside our body to repair damage to organ. They can find out cancer cells in our body.
2. They can also attack microplastics in the ocean or could clean the oil spills.
3. The best advantage is that they are controllable, stoppable and a safe system.

Disadvantages:


1. They can lead to loss of manufacturing and agricultural job.
2. Economies and market may crash.

Lot of research is still going on xenobots. According to Sam Kreigman, he and his colleagues want to increase xenobots intelligence more. They also want to use them to understand biology better which can lead to regeneration of medicine.

What is Wealthcon?

WEALTHCON® is an educational forum to teach the nuances of finance to the doctor fraternity. This idea was conceived by Dr Ramnath Ghute, a radiologist from Mumbai in 2017. This platform of Wealthcon has transformed itself into a fast growing tree with more than 20000 allopathic doctors from India as well as 12 overseas countries being actively involved in financial education of doctors.
